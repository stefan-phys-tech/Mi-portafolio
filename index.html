Topological Quantum Materials

From Theory to Simulation

1. Introduction

This work explores the field of topological quantum materials, covering quantum insulators through the study of diverse quantized Hall Effects, both theoretically and practically via computer-based condensed matter simulation.

2. Theoretical Framework

Topological Insulators & Hall Effect

The study delves into the Integer Quantum Hall Effect (IQHE) where Hall conductance is quantized. We analyzed the role of the Berry Phase and the Chern Number as topological invariants (integers) that characterize the quantized Hall conductivity.

BHZ Model

We focused on the Bernevig-Hughes-Zhang (BHZ) model for HgTe/CdTe quantum wells. In the topological regime (thickness > 6.3 nm), the band structure inverts, creating conducting edge states protected by Time Reversal Symmetry (TRS).

3. Computational Simulation

Using Python and the kwant library, we implemented the effective BHZ Hamiltonian to simulate a nanoribbon of HgTe.

  • Bulk Bands: Observed the energy gap opening in the bulk material.
  • Surface Bands: Verified the existence of "X"-shaped crossing lines within the forbidden gap (Dirac cones), indicating massless edge states.
  • Probability Density: The simulation confirmed that for states within the gap, the probability density is localized at the edges, decaying exponentially towards the center.

4. Topological Qubits

The project also reviewed the theoretical basis of Majorana Fermions and the Kitaev Chain model. Majorana Zero Modes (MZMs) in topological superconductors offer potential for robust topological quantum computing, where qubits are protected from local decoherence.

Scientific Report: ICIQ Research Stay

Uranyl Speciation in Carbonate Media

1. Introduction

Polyoxometalates (POMs) are complex molecular nanoclusters. This project applied the POMSimulator software to model the speciation of uranyl in carbonate media, treating the uranyl-carbonate system as a heteropolyoxometalate. The goal was to reproduce and validate speciation results under varying conditions.

2. Methodology

  • Preparation: Identification of chemical species and modelling using Amsterdam Density Functional (ADF) to obtain initial geometries.
  • Simulation: Used POMsimulator to calculate formation constants.
  • Scaling: Constants were scaled using Python scripts. The "Best_rmse" method (individual adjustment) proved most accurate compared to universal scaling.

3. Results & Discussion

We successfully generated speciation diagrams (distribution of species vs pH). A critical finding was the instability of the model when the carbonate-to-uranium ratio approached 10:1.

Progressive ratio testing (1:0.9 to 1:0.3) showed that valid models decreased drastically as carbonate concentration increased. However, for lower ratios, the clustered species diagrams matched experimental literature well.

4. Conclusions

The workflow confirmed that POMSimulator is a powerful tool for complex inorganic systems. The best_rmse scaling strategy is essential for accuracy. Future work involves resolving the high-ratio instability to extend the analysis to different temperatures.

Impacto de la Lluvia Ácida en Cultivos

Investigación Experimental en Alcover

1. Introducción y Contexto

La contaminación atmosférica y la lluvia ácida representan riesgos significativos para la agricultura local. Este trabajo investiga si la lluvia ácida es un factor de riesgo real para las plantaciones de trigo en la zona de Alcover, mediante un enfoque experimental riguroso.

2. Metodología Experimental

Se diseñaron tres experimentos complementarios para evaluar el impacto:

  • Experimento 1 (Monitoreo): Seguimiento de los valores de pH y conductividad del agua de lluvia real en Alcover, detectando presencia de sulfatos y nitratos.
  • Experimento 2 (Atmósfera Artificial): Creación de una atmósfera controlada para replicar y observar el proceso de acidificación del agua por contaminantes gaseosos.
  • Experimento 3 (Germinación): Estudio del efecto de diferentes concentraciones de ácido sobre la tasa de germinación de semillas de trigo.

3. Resultados y Conclusiones

El estudio proporcionó datos cuantitativos sobre la resistencia del trigo a condiciones de acidez. Se observó una correlación directa entre el aumento de la acidez y la disminución de la tasa de germinación, estableciendo umbrales de riesgo para los cultivos locales.

Este trabajo destaca por la aplicación del método científico para resolver una problemática medioambiental local, utilizando herramientas de química analítica (testers de pH, tiras reactivas) y análisis estadístico de datos biológicos.
